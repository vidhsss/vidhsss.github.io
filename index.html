Projects and Works

Ambient intelligence-based multimodal human action recognition for autonomous systems

This work presents a method based on Bi-Convolutional Recurrent Neural Network (Bi-CRNN) -based Feature Extraction and then Random Forest classification for achieving outcomes utilizing Ambient Intelligence that are at the cutting edge of human action recognition for Autonomous Robots. The auto fusion technique used has improved fusion for utilizing and processing data from various sensors. This paper has drawn comparisons with already existing algorithms for Human Action Recognition (HAR) and tried to propose a heuristic and constructive hybrid deep learning-based algorithm with an accuracy of 94.7%.

Can Deep Neural Network Mimic Human Perception

Developed methods that mitigate limitations of CNNs along with self-supervised training and their co-relation with human perception. The phenomenons like the Thatcher effect, Weber’s law, Occlusion, and Global advantage as shown by human perception are tested for various CNN architecture trained using different datasets and training methods.

Behavior Analysis in Songbird

Employed audio processing and analysis to investigate how motion and sleep exhaustion affect songbird learning. The song syllables across days and timing were separated using amplitude cutting and then further represented using Umap and t-SNE. Each day, the 2D syllable representations provided insights into the exploration or exploitation of new minima, with clusters indicating local minima and the formation of crystallized syllables

AGRI-AI

Deployed an end-to-end web application to assist farmers in administering their crop quality, suggesting crop price, and predicting the yield taking into account the soil and crop type, all by the comfort of a click of a button. Used CatBoost Classifier, Gaussian Naïve Bayes Model, MobileNet, Random Forest Regressor, and Deep bidirectional LSTM Models one for each feature in the project. The aggregate accuracy achieved by the 5 Models is 91%.

Hybrid Attention Multi-modal learning for Perceived Mental workload Classification

The project is based on the applicability of the brain-computer interface using multimodal biosignals and attention networks for quantifying cognitive load in well-validated problem-solving tasks. The proposed approach firstly models each modality through LSTM and Convolution based self-attention. Then it applies bi-modal attention to pairwise modalities and tries to learn the contributing features amongst them. It gives an accuracy of 92.3%.
